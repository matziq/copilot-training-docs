Demo 1 Prompting Best Practices ▼
📝 Presenter Note
Copilot CSA leads | Setup: m365copilot.com
Goal: Show the difference between vague and great prompts before demos get complex. Establish the pattern that runs through every subsequent demo.
  1. 1 Weak Prompt — Type the following as-is to demonstrate the generic response:
Weak Prompt (show first)
Tell me about marketing
💬 Talking Point
“You’ll get something generic. That’s the AI being polite but unhelpful. Now watch what happens when we give it context.”
  1. 2 Strong Prompt — Now use a well-structured prompt:
Strong Prompt
You are a senior marketing strategist at a large home improvement retailer. We're heading into the spring season — our biggest sales period. I need 5 data-driven talking points I could use in an executive briefing to justify increasing our media spend in the Outdoor & Garden category. Be specific and concise.
📝 Key Framework
Walk through each element:
ROLE: Tell it who it is — “You are a...”
CONTEXT: Give it the situation (spring season, home improvement, exec audience)
ASK: Be specific about format and length
ITERATE: Follow up to refine — you don’t have to start over
  1. 3 Iteration — Follow up in the same thread:
Iteration Prompt
Now rewrite these as one-sentence bullets suitable for a slide title
💬 Talking Point
“That’s the unlock. Copilot is only as smart as the brief you give it. Every demo today follows this pattern.”
Demo 2 Upload & Analyze Marketing Data ▼
📝 Presenter Note
Copilot CSA leads | Setup: Upload Spring_Campaign_Performance.xlsx to Copilot Chat.
Goal: Show they can hand Copilot a data file and have a conversation with it. Directly addresses their stated need: “team spends SO much time looking at data.”
Prompt 1 — Initial Analysis
Here is our Spring campaign performance data. Summarize the key trends you see. What categories are outperforming and which are underperforming?
💬 Talking Point
“It read the file. No formulas, no pivot tables — just a question.”
Prompt 2 — ROAS Query
Which product category has the highest ROAS, and is it trending better or worse than last week?
Prompt 3 — Budget Reallocation
If I had to cut two underperforming categories to reallocate budget toward our top performers, what would you recommend and why?
Prompt 4 — Executive Summary
Draft a 3-bullet executive summary I could send to my VP — focus on what's working, what needs attention, and a recommended action.
💬 Talking Point
“What you just saw is the equivalent of having an analyst who reads the whole file and answers your question in 10 seconds. And we haven’t touched a formula.”
Demo 3 PowerPoint Agent: Build the Exec Deck ▼
📝 Presenter Note
Copilot CSA leads | Setup: Stay in same Copilot Chat session — open the PowerPoint Agent.
Goal: “Blow their minds” moment. Takes the data just analyzed and builds a presentation. This is the PowerPoint Agent available in FREE Copilot Chat.
Build Deck Prompt
Using the marketing performance data we just reviewed, create a 5-slide executive presentation. Include: - Slide 1: Campaign overview and key metrics - Slide 2: Top performing categories with rationale - Slide 3: Underperforming categories with budget reallocation recommendations - Slide 4: Spring season outlook and opportunity - Slide 5: Recommended next steps Audience is our VP of Marketing. Tone should be confident and action-oriented.
💬 Talking Point
“This is what used to take a junior analyst a half day. We’re going to get a first draft in under a minute.”
Follow-up — Add Confidence Levels
Add a confidence level (High / Medium / Low) to each recommendation on slide 3
Follow-up — Sharpen Tone
Make the tone more confident and action-oriented throughout. Replace any passive language with direct recommendations.
💬 Talking Point
“It’s not perfect out of the box — but it’s a 10-minute revision, not a 4-hour build.”
“This is available to your team TODAY.”

Demo 7 Agents: Product Feed Enrichment ▼
📝 Presenter Note
Copilot CSA leads | Setup: Copilot Chat (licensed tenant).
Goal: Connect directly to the customer’s stated interest — automate feed, enrich product titles and descriptions at scale.

“Let me show you what’s possible when you think beyond the individual task and start thinking about workflows.”
Set Agent Context
You are a product content specialist for a large home improvement retailer. I'm going to give you a product entry with a weak title and description. Your job is to enrich it — make the title specific, include key product attributes (brand, size, material, use case), and write a 2-sentence description optimized for search and conversions.
Sample Product Entry
Product entry: - Item #: 4821 - Category: Paint - Current title: "White Paint 1 gal" - Current description: "Paint for walls." - Known attributes: Interior, flat finish, low VOC, 400 sq ft coverage
Scale Prompt — Reusable Template
Now imagine I have 500 products in this category with similarly weak titles. Write me a reusable prompt template I could use to batch-enrich all of them consistently
💬 Image Scanning Talking Point
“On your question about scanning product images — Copilot can analyze product images today. You can drop an image into Copilot Chat and ask ‘What attributes can you identify from this product image?’ That output becomes an input layer for your feed enrichment agent — closing the loop between your visual assets and your catalog data.”
📝 Copilot Studio Bridge
“The next conversation is Copilot Studio — where you define these rules once, connect them to your product feed, and run them at scale. That’s a half-day workshop on its own. We should set that up.”

Backup Plans

Contingency Notes

Contingency Scenario-Based Fallbacks ▼
If VP of Marketing Attends
Lean heavily on Work IQ / executive productivity story in Demo 4. Soften hands-on demos — give them “what does this mean for my org” over “here’s how to type a prompt.” Lead with time savings and ROI framing.
For India Team Dialed In
The async catch-up and meeting recap demos in Demo 4 land strongest for remote attendees. Call it out explicitly: “This is built for distributed teams.”
If PowerPoint Agent Has Issues
Fall back to showing the output in Copilot Chat as formatted text. Say: “This is what you’d paste into your deck as a starting point — the agent output typically goes directly into the file, but the intelligence is the same.” Still lands the core message.
On Free vs. Licensed Boundary
Stick to m365copilot.com and Teams for all Part 1 demos. Do NOT demo free Copilot inside Word/Excel/PowerPoint for this customer (large enterprise, free in-app availability not confirmed for their seat count).

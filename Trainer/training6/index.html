Segment 3 · 15:00–30:00

The Art of Prompting

~15 min
Framework The 4 Prompt Ingredients ▼
💬 Say
“The single biggest factor in getting great results from Copilot is how you write your prompt. There are 4 ingredients.”
Ingredient Meaning Example
Goal What do you want Copilot to do? “Write a summary…”
Context Why, for whom, relevant background “…for a manager who wasn’t in the meeting”
Source Which data should Copilot use? “…based on this attached file”
Expectations Format, tone, length “…in 3 bullet points, professional tone”

Compare these two prompts:

Weak Prompt
Summarize news about our company.
Strong Prompt
Summarize the key business risks mentioned in the attached Q3 report. Write 5 bullet points for an executive audience. Keep it under 150 words.
Demo 1 Email Subject Lines ▼
💬 Say
“Let’s say you’ve written an internal email about a new sustainability initiative and you want to make it stand out in the inbox.”
Prompt
Suggest 5 compelling email subject lines for the following email. Make them stand out in a crowded inbox and match a professional but energetic tone. [Paste a short email body about the initiative]
Follow-up Prompt
I like #3. Can you write a stronger opening paragraph for the email body?
📝 Debrief
Notice how we iterated — first we got subjects, then refined the content. That’s how Copilot works best: as a conversation, not a one-shot command.
Demo 2 Brainstorming ▼
💬 Say
“Copilot is an excellent thought partner when you’re stuck or need fresh ideas fast.”
Prompt
Generate 10 creative campaign ideas for promoting our new sustainability initiative to employees. Consider a mix of digital, in-person, and social formats.
Follow-up Prompt
I like ideas 2 and 7. Develop each into a one-paragraph pitch I could share with leadership.
Demo 3 File Upload: Meeting Notes Summary ▼
💬 Say
“This is one of the most powerful everyday uses — summarizing documents you don’t have time to read fully.”

Upload a meeting notes document, then type:

Prompt
Summarize the attached meeting notes into a concise executive summary. Focus on key decisions, action items, and any unresolved issues. Format as: - Decisions Made - Action Items (with owner if mentioned) - Open Questions
📝 Debrief
Two minutes vs. reading 4 pages of notes. That time adds up.
Demo 4 Writing Assistance ▼
💬 Say
“Copilot can write, edit, and improve any content you throw at it.”
Prompt
Write a compelling LinkedIn post to introduce our new [product or initiative]. Audience: industry peers and potential partners. Tone: confident and forward-looking. Length: 3-4 short paragraphs with a strong closing call to action.
Follow-up Prompt
Make it shorter — under 100 words — and lead with a surprising statistic or bold statement.

Segment 4 · 30:00–38:00

Memory & Personalization

~8 min
Demo Custom Instructions ▼
💬 Say
“Over time, Copilot learns from you. It remembers your preferences, your writing style, and your work context — so responses feel less generic and more like your assistant.”

Navigate to Settings → Work Profile / Custom Instructions in Copilot Chat.

💬 Say
“Here you can explicitly tell Copilot about yourself.”
Custom Instructions Example
I am a [your role] at [your company]. I prefer concise, plain-language responses. When writing emails or communications for me, always match a professional but approachable tone. Avoid jargon.
💬 Say
“Once you set this, every response Copilot gives you will take this into account — you won’t have to repeat it every time.”

Demo — Test it:

Prompt
Draft a short message to my team letting them know the Q2 planning meeting is moved to next Thursday at 2pm CT.
📝 Show
How the tone matches the custom instructions.

Segment 5 · 38:00–44:00

Image Generation

~6 min
Demo Generate & Refine Images ▼
💬 Say
“Copilot Chat can generate images directly from a text description — no design skills required. Great for presentations, social content, or internal comms.”
Prompt 1
Create a vibrant, professional image representing sustainability and innovation in the beverage industry. Style: modern, optimistic, suitable for a PowerPoint presentation.
Prompt 2 (Refinement)
Regenerate the image but make the color palette warmer — golden tones — and remove any text from the image.
📝 Debrief
You can iterate on images just like text. Describe what you want, adjust, and go.
💬 Tip
Save any image you like by right-clicking → Save Image As.

Prompts Cheat Sheet

Cheat Sheet Quick Reference ▼
Task Starter Prompt

Draft an email Draft an email to [recipient] about [topic]. Tone: [professional/casual]. Length: [short/medium].
Brainstorm ideas Generate 10 ideas for [goal]. Consider [constraints or context].
Edit / improve writing Rewrite the following to be more [concise/persuasive/clear]: [paste text]
Create an image Create an image of [description]. Style: [modern/vibrant/minimal]. Use for [purpose].
Meeting recap Summarize these meeting notes. List: Decisions, Action Items (with owner), Open Questions.
LinkedIn post Write a LinkedIn post about [topic]. Audience: [who]. Tone: [tone]. Max [X] words.

Section 2 — 10 min

Introduction to Copilot Chat

Talking Points What Is Copilot Chat? ▼

Microsoft 365 Copilot Chat is an AI-powered chat assistant built into Microsoft 365 — and available to you without any additional license. It’s powered by large language models (the same technology behind ChatGPT) but with one critical difference: when you use it in Work mode, it’s connected to your organization’s data through Microsoft Graph. That means it can pull from your emails, your calendar, your Teams conversations, and your SharePoint documents. It’s not just a generic chatbot — it understands your work context.

Key Message: “Think of it as a brilliant colleague who has read everything you’ve written and attended every meeting you’ve been in.”

Talking Points Where Can You Access Copilot Chat? ▼

Copilot Chat is available in three places — all without needing an additional license:

Access Point Details
Teams Click the Copilot icon in the left sidebar — available in both desktop and web
M365.com Click Copilot from the left navigation or app launcher — works in any browser
Copilot.com Sign in with your work account for the full Copilot Chat experience
📝 Trainer Tip
Note: The embedded Copilot experiences inside Outlook, Word, and PowerPoint are separate features that require a Microsoft 365 Copilot license — those are not what we’re covering today.
Talking Points Copilot Chat vs. App-Specific Copilot ▼
Copilot Chat (No Extra License — Today’s Focus) App-Specific Copilot (License Required)
General-purpose AI assistant — no additional license required Specialized experiences embedded in Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook
Available in Teams sidebar, M365.com, and Copilot.com Requires a separate Microsoft 365 Copilot license
Work mode: access your emails, calendar, Teams, SharePoint via Microsoft Graph Context-aware within each app (drafts from your doc, analyzes your data)
Web mode: web-grounded answers and general AI assistance In-app charting, formulas, and real-time meeting summaries
Upload files or reference OneDrive/SharePoint docs Deeply integrated with the document you’re working in

Today we’re focusing entirely on Copilot Chat, which gives you powerful AI assistance right now.

Talking Points What Copilot Can & Cannot Do ▼
What Copilot CAN Do Limitations to Know
Draft, rewrite, and summarize text Not 100% accurate — always verify (AI can hallucinate)
Answer questions about your emails, meetings, and files (Work mode) Cannot access data you don’t have permission to see
Create structured content (agendas, plans, reports) Does not take action unless you confirm
Translate content and adjust tone Cannot replace human judgment on complex decisions
Analyze uploaded files and surface insights May not have real-time data unless web search is enabled
Search the web when web grounding is enabled Your data is never used to train the AI model

Data Privacy: Your data stays within your Microsoft 365 tenant. It’s not shared externally and doesn’t train the model. Follow your company’s AI usage policy.

Section 4 — 10 min

Writing Effective Prompts

Framework The GCSE Prompting Framework ▼

The single biggest factor in getting good results from Copilot is how you write your prompts. Use the GCSE framework:

Element What It Means Example
Goal What do you want Copilot to do? “Write an email…”
Context What background does it need? “…to my project team about Q1 progress…”
Source What data should it use? “…based on our last meeting notes…”
Expectation What format or length do you want? “…in 3 short paragraphs, professional tone.”
Example Weak vs. Strong Prompts ▼
Weak Prompt Strong Prompt
Help me with my email. Write a 3-paragraph email to my manager summarizing project status, risks, and next week’s priorities. Use a professional tone.
Summarize this. Summarize this 20-page report in 5 bullet points. Focus on conclusions and recommendations.
Write a meeting agenda. Create a 30-minute project kick-off agenda for a technical team. Include time slots and a 5-minute Q&A at the end.

Stronger prompts include who the audience is, what tone to use, and what format you want. This takes 10 extra seconds and dramatically improves the result.

Demo Prompt Refinement ▼

You rarely need a perfect prompt on the first try. Copilot is designed for back-and-forth conversation:

Strategy Example
Change the format “Can you put that in a table instead?”
Ask for more detail “Expand on the second point about timeline risks”
Adjust the tone “Make this sound more casual and friendly”
Request shorter version “Shorten this to 3 bullet points maximum”
Try a different angle “Approach this from the customer’s perspective instead”
📝 Demo Instructions
  1. Refer back to a previous response (or generate a new one)
  2. Type the refinement prompt below
  3. Show how Copilot transforms the output
  4. Try: “Now make it more concise — max 3 bullets per section.”
Demo Prompt
Reformat this as a bulleted list with bold headers.
Talking Points Common Mistakes to Avoid ▼
Mistake Instead, Try…
Too vague — “Help me with work” Be specific about the task, output type, and audience
No context — “Write a proposal” Include the project name, audience, goals, and constraints
Treating it like a search engine — “Q2 report budget numbers” Ask in full sentences: “What were the Q2 budget figures from my Finance team report?”
Expecting magic on complex tasks — “Write my entire presentation” Break it down: start with an outline, then draft each section
Not verifying output — Copying directly Always review and fact-check key figures, dates, and names
Giving up after one attempt — “It didn’t understand me” Refine your prompt — add more context or rephrase

Working with Files in Copilot

Talking Points Why Files Are a Game-Changer ▼

You can bring your documents right into the conversation — contracts, research reports, spreadsheets, meeting notes. Ask Copilot Chat to work with that content directly. No copy-pasting needed.

How to attach: Click the attachment icon in the prompt bar. You can browse your OneDrive, SharePoint, or upload from your computer.

What you can do: Summarize documents, extract key information, compare multiple files, draft content from a brief, analyze spreadsheet data, and ask Q&A about documents.

Demo Working with Spreadsheet Data ▼

Copilot can read structured data from Excel (.xlsx) files attached to the chat. Ask it to identify trends, outliers, top performers, or calculate differences. Great for turning raw numbers into insights.

Best for: sales reports, budget summaries, KPI tracking files, survey results.

Demo Prompt
Analyze this sales data and tell me which region had the highest growth.
Alternative
Write a 3-sentence narrative summary of these monthly figures.

Note: For complex Excel analysis (pivot tables, formulas, charts), use Copilot inside Excel directly for best results.

Talking Points File Tips & Limitations ▼
Topic Details
Supported Types Word (.docx), PDF (.pdf), PowerPoint (.pptx), Excel (.xlsx), Plain text (.txt)
File Size Limit Up to approximately 4 MB per file
Upload Method Browse OneDrive, SharePoint, or upload from your computer via the attachment icon
Confidentiality Only share documents with Copilot Chat if your company’s policy permits it. When in doubt, check with IT.

Section 7 — 10 min

Best Practices & Power User Tips

Tips Top 10 Power User Tips ▼
  1. 1 Be specific — include context, audience, and desired format in every prompt
  2. 2 Use the GCSE framework — Goal, Context, Source, Expectation
  3. 3 Iterate — refine your prompt if the first response isn’t quite right
  4. 4 Start fresh chats — for unrelated topics, to avoid confusion
  5. 5 Always review — verify AI-generated content before sharing or submitting
  6. 6 Start with Copilot Chat — for general tasks, then explore licensed Copilot in apps when available
  7. 7 Upload files — instead of copy-pasting; Copilot handles large documents better
  8. 8 Use web search — enable web grounding for up-to-date research and current information
  9. 9 Bookmark prompts — that work well and build a personal prompt library
  10. 10 Report issues & share feedback — help improve Copilot for your organization
Talking Points Data Privacy & Security ▼
  • Data stays in your tenant — Copilot Chat operates within your Microsoft 365 tenant boundary
  • Your data is not used to train Copilot — Microsoft does not use your organizational data, prompts, or responses to improve the AI models
  • Permissions are respected — if someone doesn’t have access to a file, Copilot Chat won’t surface that file to them
  • Audit logging — Copilot Chat keeps an audit log of interactions for compliance purposes
  • Follow your data classification policies — do not input highly confidential or regulated data not approved for AI processing
Talking Points What Copilot Is Great For (and What It’s Not) ▼
Great For Less Suited For
First drafts of documents and emails Final decisions requiring human judgment
Summarizing long documents Sensitive HR or legal matters
Brainstorming and ideation Advice requiring up-to-the-minute accuracy
Reformatting content Sharing highly confidential data
Analyzing structured data for insights Replacing expert review or approval

Key Message: “Think of it as a brilliant intern — incredibly helpful, but you’re still the expert.”

Talking Points Copilot vs. Search vs. Ask a Colleague ▼
Use Copilot When… Use Search When… Ask a Colleague When…
Drafting, summarizing, or rewriting content Finding a specific document you know exists The question requires organizational judgment
Pulling insights from emails, meetings, and files (Work mode) Looking up a policy, form, or process doc You need someone who was “in the room”
Generating first drafts quickly Browsing current news or external content The topic requires accountability or sign-off
Comparing or analyzing documents Finding a known file by name or keyword Building relationship matters more than the answer
Creating agendas, plans, or structured content Getting precise results from structured databases The topic is sensitive or confidential

Section 2 — 10 min

Introduction to Copilot

Talking Points What Is Microsoft 365 Copilot? ▼

Microsoft 365 Copilot is an AI assistant built directly into the tools you already use every day — Teams, Outlook, Word, Excel, SharePoint. It’s powered by large language models (the same technology behind ChatGPT) but with one critical difference: it’s connected to your organization’s data through Microsoft Graph.

Key Message: “Think of it as a brilliant colleague who has read everything you’ve written and attended every meeting you’ve been in.”

Talking Points Where Can You Use Copilot? ▼
  • Teams — Chat sidebar
  • Outlook — Draft emails and summarize threads
  • Word & PowerPoint — Draft and refine content
  • Copilot Chat — General-purpose assistant at M365.com or Teams sidebar (today’s focus)
Talking Points Copilot Chat vs. App-Specific Copilot ▼

Copilot Chat = General-purpose assistant — like having a browser open and you can ask it anything.

App-specific Copilots (Word, Excel, etc.) = Specialized for what you’re doing in that app at that moment.

Today we’re focusing on Copilot Chat, which gives you the most flexibility.

Talking Points What Copilot Can & Cannot Do ▼

Copilot is incredibly powerful, but it’s not perfect. It can make mistakes — what the industry calls “hallucinations.”

  • Always review and verify output before sending or acting on it
  • Your data stays within your Microsoft tenant — it’s not shared externally and doesn’t train the model
  • Follow your company’s AI usage policy

Section 4 — 15 min

Writing Effective Prompts

Framework The GCSE Prompting Framework ▼

The single biggest factor in getting good results from Copilot is how you write your prompts. Use the GCSE framework:

Element What It Means Example
Goal What do you want Copilot to do? “Write an email…”
Context What background does it need? “…to my project team about Q1 progress…”
Source What data should it use? “…based on our last meeting notes…”
Expectation What format or length do you want? “…in 3 short paragraphs, professional tone.”
Example Weak vs. Strong Prompts ▼
Weak Prompt Strong Prompt
Help me with my email. Write a 3-paragraph email to my manager summarizing project status, risks, and next week’s priorities. Use a professional tone.
Summarize this. Summarize this 20-page report in 5 bullet points. Focus on conclusions and recommendations.
Write a meeting agenda. Create a 30-minute project kick-off agenda for a technical team. Include time slots and a 5-minute Q&A at the end.

Stronger prompts include who the audience is, what tone to use, and what format you want. This takes 10 extra seconds and dramatically improves the result.

Demo Prompt Refinement ▼

Copilot is designed for iteration. Use follow-ups like:

  • “Make this more concise.”
  • “Change the tone to be more formal.”
  • “Add a section about risks.”
  • “Format this as a table instead.”
📝 Demo Instructions
  1. Refer back to a previous response (or generate a new one)
  2. Type the refinement prompt below
  3. Show how Copilot transforms the output
  4. Try: “Now make it more concise — max 3 bullets per section.”
Demo Prompt
Reformat this as a bulleted list with bold headers.
Talking Points Common Mistakes to Avoid ▼
  • Too vague — “help me with this” won’t get you far
  • Forgetting format — You’ll get a wall of text when you wanted a table
  • No context — Copilot doesn’t know your business without you telling it
  • Over-trusting — Always read and verify before using

Section 5 — 20 min

Working with Files in Copilot

Talking Points Why Files Are a Game-Changer ▼

You can bring your documents right into the conversation — contracts, research reports, spreadsheets, meeting notes. Ask Copilot to work with that content directly. No copy-pasting needed.

Demo How to Attach a File ▼
📝 Demo Instructions
  1. Click the + (plus) icon in the Copilot Chat prompt bar
  2. Select “Browse OneDrive” or “Upload from this device”
  3. Select a sample document (prepare a .docx or .pdf in advance)
  4. Confirm it appears as a chip/badge above the prompt bar
  5. Point out the file is now in the conversation context
Demo Summarize a Document ▼

The most common use case. Attach a long report and ask Copilot to distill it for you. A 50-page report? Done in seconds.

Demo Prompt
Summarize this document in 5 bullet points highlighting the most important decisions.
Demo Extract Information ▼

Pull specific data points from long documents — action items, dates, stakeholders, deadlines.

Demo Prompt
What are the action items and who owns each one?
Follow-Up
List all dates and deadlines mentioned in this document.
Demo Compare Multiple Documents ▼

Attach two or more files for comparisons — contracts, proposals, or different drafts. Useful for legal reviews, vendor comparisons, or reviewing multiple drafts.

📝 Demo Instructions
  1. Start a new Copilot Chat session
  2. Attach two sample documents (e.g., two versions of a proposal)
  3. Type the prompt below and show the comparison
Demo Prompt
What are the main differences between these two documents?
Demo Create Content from a File ▼

Use the file as raw material to create something new — emails, status updates, discussion questions.

Demo Prompt
Turn these meeting notes into a professional email update for stakeholders.
Follow-Up
Now make the tone more formal and add a subject line.
Talking Points File Tips & Limitations ▼
Topic Details
Supported Types Word (.docx), PDF (.pdf), PowerPoint (.pptx), Excel (.xlsx), Plain text (.txt)
File Size Limit Up to approximately 4 MB per file
Storage Files should be stored in OneDrive or SharePoint for best results; local files need to be uploaded
Confidentiality Only share documents with Copilot if your company’s policy permits it. When in doubt, check with IT.

Section 7 — 5 min

Best Practices & Power User Tips

Tips Top 10 Power User Tips ▼
  1. 1 Be specific — include audience, tone, and format in your prompt
  2. 2 Iterate — follow up to refine (“Make it shorter,” “Use a table”)
  3. 3 Always review — verify facts before sending or acting on Copilot output
  4. 4 Use follow-up questions — Copilot remembers context within a session
  5. 5 Try “What else?” — ask Copilot to expand, add examples, or suggest alternatives
  6. 6 Name your audience — “Write this for a non-technical executive” changes everything
  7. 7 Ask for options — “Give me 3 different versions of this email”
  8. 8 Use it for brainstorming — “Give me 10 ideas for…”
  9. 9 Check privacy — follow your organization’s AI usage policy
  10. 10 Share what works — contribute your best prompts to your team’s shared library
Talking Points Data Privacy & Security ▼
  • Copilot operates within your Microsoft 365 tenant boundary
  • Your data is not used to train AI models
  • It respects your existing security and compliance settings — if someone doesn’t have access to a file, Copilot won’t surface that file to them
  • Copilot keeps an audit log of interactions for compliance
Talking Points What Copilot Is Great For (and What It’s Not) ▼
Great For Less Suited For
First drafts Final decisions
Summarization Sensitive HR situations
Analysis & brainstorming Legal advice
Reformatting content Up-to-the-minute data accuracy

Key Message: “Think of it as a brilliant intern — incredibly helpful, but you’re still the expert.”

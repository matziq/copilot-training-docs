Scenario 1 of 4

Triage Your Inbox

~3 min
📝 Setup
App: Outlook for Web or Desktop
Setup: Have Outlook open with your inbox visible. Make sure you have at least 10–15 unread emails from the past 2 days.
Demo Step-by-Step Instructions ▼
  1. 1 Open Outlook and go to your Inbox
  2. 2 Click the Copilot icon in the toolbar (or press Ctrl+Shift+.)
  3. 3 Type the following prompt:
Prompt to Use
Summarize my unread emails from the last 48 hours. Highlight anything that looks urgent, pull out any action items assigned to me, and tell me what I can probably deprioritize.

What to Expect

  • Copilot will return a bulleted summary grouped by urgency
  • Action items will be called out separately
  • You can follow up with: “Draft a reply to the most urgent email”
💬 Talking Points
“This is what Monday morning looks like — instead of reading 47 emails, you get this in 10 seconds”
“Notice how it surfaced the action items automatically — no more digging”
“You can ask follow-up questions, it’s a conversation”
⚠️ Facilitator Tips
  • If the inbox is sparse, say: “In a real scenario you’d have many more emails — the power scales with volume”
  • If Copilot is slow, keep talking about the use case while it loads
  • Backup: have a screenshot ready of a sample output

Scenario 2 of 4

Prep for a Big Meeting

~4 min
📝 Setup
App: Microsoft Outlook (Copilot in Outlook)
Setup: Have Outlook open. Ideally, have a few recent emails related to Q1 results or an upcoming meeting topic.
Demo Step-by-Step Instructions ▼
  1. 1 Open Outlook and click the Copilot icon in the toolbar
  2. 2 Type the following prompt:
Prompt to Use
I have a QBR with my VP tomorrow morning. Please check my last 5 emails related to our Q1 results. Give me a 5-point briefing I can use to prepare — include any risks or open questions I should be ready to address.

What to Expect

  • Copilot will pull from your recent emails
  • Returns a structured briefing with context, key points, and risks
  • Follow up with: “What questions should I be prepared to answer?”
💬 Talking Points
“This replaces 30 minutes of digging through emails and notes”
“It’s grounding the response in YOUR actual data — your real emails”
“The follow-up prompt is where it gets really powerful”
⚠️ Facilitator Tips
  • If the results are sparse, say: “The richer your data, the better this gets — over time Copilot learns your work patterns”
  • Show the follow-up prompt to demonstrate the conversational nature

Scenario 3 of 4

Draft a Report in Word

~4 min
📝 Setup
App: Microsoft Word (web or desktop)
Setup: Have a blank Word document open. Optionally: have a related document in OneDrive to reference.
Demo Step-by-Step Instructions ▼
  1. 1 Open a new Word document
  2. 2 Click Draft with Copilot (appears at the top of a blank doc)
  3. 3 Type the following prompt:
Prompt to Use
Draft a one-page project status report for the Azure Migration project. Write it for a VP-level audience — clear, concise, executive tone. Include three sections: (1) Current Status — on track with Phase 1 complete, Phase 2 delayed 2 days due to vendor issue; (2) Key Risks — vendor dependency, team bandwidth; (3) Next Steps — Phase 2 completion by March 28, stakeholder review scheduled for March 30.

What to Expect

  • Copilot generates a full structured document
  • Formatted with headings, bullet points, professional tone
  • You can then ask: “Make the risks section more specific and add a recommended mitigation for each”
💬 Talking Points
“This took about 30 seconds — a report that might have taken an hour”
“You gave it the facts — it handled the structure, format, and language”
“Notice the tone is exactly VP-level — that’s the ‘audience’ instruction working”
⚠️ Facilitator Tips
  • If the output is too long, show the follow-up: “Make this more concise — cut it to half the length”
  • Use the Rewrite feature to show inline editing if time permits

Scenario 4 of 4

Data Insights in Excel

~3 min
📝 Setup
App: Microsoft Excel (desktop or web, with Copilot enabled)
Setup: Have a spreadsheet open with at least 20–30 rows of sales or project data. Columns should include: Region, Month, Revenue, Target, Prior Year.
Demo Step-by-Step Instructions ▼
  1. 1 Open your Excel file
  2. 2 Click Copilot in the Home ribbon
  3. 3 Type the following prompt:
Prompt to Use
Analyze this sales data and tell me: which 3 regions had the biggest gap between actual and target, what's the overall month-over-month trend, and which chart type would best communicate this to a senior leadership audience?

What to Expect

  • Copilot returns a narrative summary of the analysis
  • Identifies underperformers with specific numbers
  • Recommends chart type with reasoning
  • Follow up: “Create that chart and highlight the underperforming regions in red”
💬 Talking Points
“No formulas written, no pivot tables built — just a question”
“It’s not just showing you the answer, it’s explaining the insight”
“The chart recommendation with ‘why’ is what used to require a data analyst”
⚠️ Facilitator Tips
  • If Copilot can’t analyze (no table formatted data), say: “Make sure your data is in a table format — Copilot needs structured data to work with”
  • Show the formula suggestion feature as a quick bonus: “Write a formula that calculates the % gap between actual and target for each row”

Best Practices

Tips for a Great Demo

Tips General Delivery ▼
  • Always have a backup screenshot in case of connectivity issues
  • Slow is smooth, smooth is fast — don’t rush through prompts
  • Encourage participants to try prompts on their own devices during the demo
  • When something doesn’t work perfectly, say “This is where you’d refine the prompt” — model the iteration behavior
  • Leave 2 minutes at the end for “your turn” — give them 1 prompt to try themselves
Troubleshooting Handling Common Issues ▼
Situation What to Do
Copilot is slow to respond Keep talking — explain what Copilot is doing and why it matters. Don’t let silence fill the room.
Output isn’t what you expected Say “This is a great example of iterating a prompt” — then refine and re-run.
Connectivity drops Switch to your backup screenshot. Narrate what you’d have shown.
Inbox / data is sparse Acknowledge it: “In production you’d have much richer data — the results scale with volume.”
Participants get ahead of you Encourage it! Ask them to share what they tried and what happened.

Prompt Crafting Principles

Principles Key Techniques ▼
  1. 1 Be specific about audience: “Write this for a VP” or “…for a technical team”
  2. 2 Specify format: “Use bullet points”, “Keep it to one page”, “Write in table format”
  3. 3 Give context: The more background you give, the better the output
  4. 4 Iterate: Show that refining prompts is normal — not a failure
  5. 5 Use follow-up prompts: Show Copilot as a conversation, not a one-shot tool

Personalization

Memory & Custom Instructions

📝 Presenter Note
Walk participants through Settings → Personalization → Custom Instructions. This is where Copilot learns their preferences and applies them to every response.
Guide Custom Instructions Content ▼

Core Principles

  • Copilot prioritizes accuracy, clarity, and trust
  • Always distinguish verified facts from inferred reasoning
  • Never present assumptions or context-based reasoning as fact
  • Avoid cascade errors by rechecking each step before responding
  • Think deeply before answering
  • When uncertain, ask clarifying questions or present 2–3 vetted options with pros and cons

Tone by Audience

Audience Tone Example Language
Internal / Collaborative Collaborative, slightly informal, pragmatic “Thanks for sharing.” “Would it work to proceed this way?”
Customer / External Professional, courteous, reassuring “Looking forward to discussing the way forward.” “Sounds like a great opportunity.”

Content Labels

Label Meaning
[Verified] Facts confirmed by authoritative sources
[Inference] Logical conclusions based on context
[Confidence] Confidence cues: “Likely”, “Based on observed patterns”
[Unverified] Claims not yet confirmed
[Glossary] Acronyms clarified on first use

Output Guidelines

  • Always include a TLDR at the top of emails and documents
  • Present information in logical order: verified facts first, inferences second, recommendations last
  • Use clear section headings
  • Be concise while preserving precision
  • Never guess or fill gaps with plausible language — state clearly when information is missing
  • Search enterprise content first: files, emails, chats, meetings, transcripts
  • For legal or regulatory topics: verify jurisdiction and effective dates
  • For time-sensitive topics: check date stamps and warn about staleness
💬 Talking Point
“Custom instructions are like training Copilot to think the way you do. Set them once, and every response reflects your standards.”

Power Tips

Additional Useful Prompts

Prompt Plain Text Output ▼

When you want the output to be just a block of text (no formatting):

Prompt
Output the results only as a block of plain text. Do not include any other text. Always treat the block of plain text as a block of code that uses text as the code language. Always include a copy button to copy the contents of the box. Do not include sources.
💬 Talking Point
“This is great when you need clean output you can paste directly into an email or document — no extra formatting to strip out.”
Prompt Get the Resultant Prompt ▼

Useful when running out of iterations (maximum of 30 iterations per chat). Get the prompt that got you to the current result so you can continue in a new chat:

Prompt
What is the resultant prompt so far?
💬 Talking Point
“If you hit the 30-iteration limit, use this to capture your work. Paste it into a new chat and pick up right where you left off.”
Prompt Blocking Off Calendar ▼

Quickly block time on your calendar without switching apps:

Prompt
Place the following hold on my calendar. Call it 'HOLD: Important Meeting' No agenda, no attendees. Just trying to block time that I offered for a QBR. All day event. The date is 6/15/2026.
💬 Talking Point
“This is a quick win — block calendar time without leaving the Copilot chat. Customize the title, date, and type as needed.”

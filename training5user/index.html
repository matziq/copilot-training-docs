Part 1 · 15 min

What Makes a Great Copilot Prompt

~15 min
Concept Why Prompting Matters ▼

Copilot is only as good as what you ask.

  • Vague prompts → generic, unhelpful responses
  • Specific prompts → save hours every week
Quality Description
MEH Vague one-liner
OK Some context added
WOW Goal + Context + Format + Source
Framework The 4 Elements of a Great Prompt ▼
  1. 1 Goal — What do you want Copilot to do?
  2. 2 Context — Who’s the audience? What’s the background?
  3. 3 Expectations — Format, length, tone, constraints
  4. 4 Source — Which data, files, or emails to use
Examples Prompt Makeover: Before & After ▼

Email

Before (Vague)
Write an email about the project
After (Great)
Draft a concise 3-paragraph email to my project stakeholders summarizing this week's progress on the Azure migration, noting the 2-day delay in Phase 2, and requesting feedback by Friday. Tone: professional but direct.

Meeting

Before (Vague)
Prep me for my meeting
After (Great)
I have a 30-minute 1:1 with my manager Cristi Bennett tomorrow. Review my last 3 emails to her, any action items she's assigned me, and my calendar for this week. Give me a 5-bullet brief.

Document

Before (Vague)
Write a summary
After (Great)
Summarize the key decisions and open questions from /Finance Q1 Review.docx in bullet format, no longer than one page, for an exec audience.

M365 Copilot Tips & Tricks by App

~10 min
Outlook Outlook Copilot ▼
  1. 1Summarize threads — Catch up on a 50-message thread in 10 seconds
  2. 2Draft with context — Pull in meeting notes, prior emails, and files
  3. 3Coaching — Get tone and clarity feedback before you hit send
  4. 4Triage your inbox — Prioritize what matters, ignore what doesn’t
Pro Tip
Summarize this thread and tell me what action I need to take
Teams Teams Copilot ▼
  1. 1Meeting recap — Get a summary + action items after any meeting
  2. 2Catch up mid-meeting — Join late? Ask “What did I miss?”
  3. 3Chat intelligence — Search across chats to find past decisions
  4. 4Draft a reply — Let Copilot suggest your response in any chat
Pro Tip
What decisions were made and what are the open items?
Word Word Copilot ▼
  1. 1Draft from scratch — Give an outline, get a full draft
  2. 2Rewrite & improve — Select any section, ask for a rewrite
  3. 3Summarize long docs — Get a TL;DR for any document
  4. 4Reference other files — Pull data from Excel or another Word doc

Rewrite this section to be more concise and executive-ready
PowerPoint PowerPoint Copilot ▼
  1. 1Create from prompt — Describe your topic, get a full deck
  2. 2Add slides from Word — Turn a Word doc into a slide
  3. 3Speaker notes — Auto-generate notes for any slide
  4. 4Design ideas — Get layout suggestions instantly
Pro Tip
Create a 5-slide deck based on /Q1 Report.docx
Excel Excel Copilot ▼
  1. 1Formula help — Describe what you need, Copilot writes the formula
  2. 2Data insights — Ask “What are the top trends in this data?”
  3. 3Python in Excel — Run advanced analysis with natural language
  4. 4Charts — Ask Copilot to visualize your data automatically

Demo: Real-World Scenarios

~15 min

Follow along on your own device!

Demo 1 Triage Your Inbox — Outlook ▼

Scenario: Monday morning, 47 unread emails

Live Prompt
Summarize my unread emails from the last 48 hours. Highlight anything urgent, flag action items, and tell me what I can ignore.

Expected Outcome

  • Urgent items surfaced immediately — no inbox scrolling
  • Action items extracted and listed by priority
  • Low-priority threads identified for bulk archive
  • Time saved: 20–30 minutes of manual triage
Demo 2 Prep for a Big Meeting — Teams + Outlook ▼

Scenario: QBR with your VP in 30 minutes

Live Prompt
I have a QBR with my VP in 30 minutes. Check my last 5 emails with her, my recent Teams messages in the project channel, and my calendar. Give me a 5-point brief with any risks I should flag.

Expected Outcome

  • 5-bullet executive brief ready in under 60 seconds
  • Risks and open items surfaced from across channels
  • You walk in prepared — not scrambling for context
  • Cross-app: emails + Teams + calendar, unified view
Demo 3 Draft a Report in Minutes — Word ▼

Scenario: Need a project status report by EOD

Live Prompt
Draft a one-page project status report for the Azure Migration project. Pull from /Project Notes.docx and the last 3 emails with subject 'Azure Migration'. Include: current status, blockers, next steps. Audience: VP level.

Expected Outcome

  • Full draft generated in seconds — grounded in your files
  • Proper structure: status, blockers, next steps
  • VP-ready tone applied automatically
  • You edit and refine instead of starting from scratch
Demo 4 Unlock Hidden Data Insights — Excel ▼

Scenario: Monthly sales data, need quick insights

Live Prompt
Look at this sales data and tell me: which 3 regions underperformed vs. last quarter, what's the YoY trend, and suggest a chart that would work best for an exec presentation.

Expected Outcome

  • Underperforming regions identified instantly
  • YoY trend analysis with natural language explanation
  • Chart recommendation matched to exec audience
  • Python in Excel runs complex analysis without formulas

Best Practices & The Copilot Playbook

~10 min
Best Practices Top 8 Copilot Best Practices ▼
  1. 1Always give context — who, what, why
  2. 2Specify your audience and format
  3. 3Iterate — refine prompts when first answer isn’t right
  4. 4Reference specific files with /
  5. 5Review everything before sending
  6. 6Use Copilot to catch your own mistakes
  7. 7Start every morning with a briefing
  8. 8Share great prompts with your team
Pitfalls Common Pitfalls to Avoid ▼
Don’t Do Instead
Send without reviewing Always review AI-generated content
Use vague one-liners Add context, format, and audience
Repeat the same failing prompt Change one variable and retry
Rely on Copilot for facts Verify statistics and quotes
Ignore the /file reference Ground Copilot in YOUR data
Playbook The Copilot Playbook ▼
  1. 1START — Replace 1 manual task per day with Copilot
  2. 2BUILD — Create a personal prompt library
  3. 3SCALE — Share wins, build team playbooks
💬 Remember
The teams that win with AI aren’t the ones with the fanciest tools. They’re the ones who build habits.

New & Coming Soon

~10 min
Available Now Edit with Copilot ▼

Inline AI editing directly within your documents. Select text, choose an action, Copilot rewrites in place.

  • Rewrite for tone, length, or clarity
  • Expand or condense any selection
  • Fix grammar and polish language
  • Accept, reject, or refine suggestions

Example

Before “The thing about this project is that it has been going on for a while and there have been a lot of issues.”
Copilot action Make this concise and professional
After “This project has experienced ongoing delays and significant challenges.”

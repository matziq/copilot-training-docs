Step 2

The 4 Ingredients of a Great Prompt

Framework Prompt Ingredients ▼

A good prompt includes four elements:

Ingredient What it means Quick example
Goal What do you want Copilot to do? “Write a summary…”
Context Why, for whom, what’s the background? “…for my manager who missed the meeting”
Source What data should Copilot reference? “…based on the attached file”
Expectations Format, tone, length “…in 3 bullets, professional tone, under 100 words”
Example Weak vs. Strong Prompt ▼
Weak Prompt
Summarize news about our company.
Strong Prompt
Summarize the key business risks in the attached Q3 report. Write 5 bullet points for an executive audience. Keep it under 150 words.

Step 3

Try These Prompts Today

Email Help Email Subject Lines ▼
Prompt
Suggest 5 compelling subject lines for the following email. Make them stand out and use a professional but energetic tone. [paste email body]
Brainstorming Campaign Ideas ▼
Prompt
Generate 10 creative campaign ideas for promoting our new sustainability initiative to employees. Consider digital, in-person, and social formats.
Meeting Summary Summarize Meeting Notes (attach a file) ▼
Prompt
Summarize the attached meeting notes into a concise executive summary. List: Decisions Made, Action Items (with owner if mentioned), Open Questions.
Writing LinkedIn Post ▼
Prompt
Write a LinkedIn post about [topic]. Audience: industry peers and partners. Tone: confident and forward-looking. Max 100 words, lead with a bold opening statement.
Image Image Generation ▼
Prompt
Create a vibrant, professional image representing sustainability and innovation in the beverage industry. Style: modern, optimistic, suitable for a PowerPoint presentation.

Step 5

Make Copilot Smarter Over Time

Setup Custom Instructions ▼

Go to Settings → Work Profile / Custom Instructions in Copilot Chat and tell Copilot about yourself:

Custom Instructions Example
I am a [your role] at [your company]. I prefer concise, plain-language responses. When writing emails or communications for me, always match a professional but approachable tone. Avoid jargon.

You only need to set this once — Copilot will apply it to all future responses automatically.

Tips to Build the Habit

Tips Daily Habits ▼
  1. 1Start small: use Copilot for one task per day
  2. 2Iterate: ask follow-up questions to refine the response
  3. 3Try Work mode for internal tasks and Web mode for general research
  4. 4Share prompts that work well with your colleagues

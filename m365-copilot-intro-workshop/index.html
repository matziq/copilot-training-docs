Before you begin

No installation needed — just a couple of quick checks so the demos work smoothly.

1. Sign in

Use a Microsoft 365 account that has a Copilot license. The Copilot icon appears in the ribbon or top bar of each app.

2. Open Copilot Chat

Find Copilot at microsoft365.com/chat, in Teams, or via the Copilot icon in Word, Outlook, Excel, and PowerPoint.

3. Use safe demo data

Have a sample document, an email thread, and a small Excel table ready. Avoid sensitive content during the class.

Demo 1 — Copilot Chat

Your starting point for everyday work. Ask in plain language; Copilot pulls from your emails, chats, meetings, and files.

Catch up on your week
Summarize everything I worked on this week and group it by project. Highlight anything that still needs a reply.
Find & summarize a file
Find the latest version of the Q3 planning document and give me the three key takeaways.
Draft a status update
Draft a friendly status update I can post in Teams about the migration project, based on my recent files and chats.

✅ Expected result: a concise, grounded summary with links to the real items it referenced.

Demo 2 — Copilot in Word

Go from blank page to first draft, then refine. Open Copilot from the ribbon or press the Copilot icon in the margin.

Draft from scratch
Draft a one-page project brief for the website refresh aimed at executive stakeholders. Include goals, scope, and a timeline.
Summarize a document
Summarize this document into five bullet points a busy manager can scan.
Rewrite a section
Rewrite this section to be more concise and confident, keeping the original meaning.

✅ Expected result: an editable draft you can accept, regenerate, or refine with a follow-up prompt.

Demo 3 — Copilot in Outlook

Tame the inbox: summarize long threads and draft replies in the right tone with Coaching.

Summarize a thread
Summarize this long email thread and list the open questions directed at me.
Draft a reply
Draft a polite reply declining this meeting and proposing two alternative times next week.
Coach my draft
Coach me on this draft: make it sound more professional and a little warmer.

✅ Expected result: a ready-to-edit reply plus feedback on tone, clarity, and sentiment.

Demo 4 — Copilot in Teams

Never take manual notes again. Recap meetings and surface what was assigned to you.

Recap a meeting
Recap this meeting: key decisions, action items with owners, and any unresolved questions.
Catch up on a chat
What did I miss while I was away from this chat? Summarize the important points and anything that needs my input.

✅ Expected result: a structured recap with owners and due dates you can copy into a task or email.

Demo 5 — Excel & PowerPoint

Turn data into insight, and ideas into slides.

Excel — analyze a table
Analyze this table and tell me the top three trends. Then add a column that flags rows where revenue is below target.
PowerPoint — build a deck
Create a 6-slide deck introducing Microsoft 365 Copilot for a non-technical audience, with a title slide and speaker notes.
PowerPoint — declutter a slide
Make this slide less crowded — split it into two and keep the key message.

✅ Expected result: insights and a flag column in Excel; a first-draft deck you can refine in PowerPoint.

Prompting that works

A simple formula gets better results — and refining beats restarting.

The G-C-S-E formula

Goal + Context + Source + Expectation. Tell Copilot what you want, the background, what to use, and the format or length.

"Draft a customer email (goal) about our delayed shipment (context) using the thread with Contoso (source). Keep it under 120 words and apologetic but confident (expectation)."

Refine, don't restart

If the first answer isn't right, tell Copilot what to change: "shorter," "more formal," "add a next-steps section." Iteration is where the magic happens.

Always review

Copilot can make mistakes. Check facts, figures, and tone before you send or share. You're the editor in chief.

Key takeaways

Three things to remember when you leave today.

1

Start in Chat

Copilot Chat is the easiest on-ramp. Ask it to catch you up, find things, and draft — then move into the apps.

2

Be specific, then refine

Use Goal + Context + Source + Expectation, and iterate on the answer instead of starting over.

3

You stay in control

Copilot respects your existing permissions and keeps your data in your tenant. Always review before you send.

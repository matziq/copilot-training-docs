Overview

What Is Copilot Chat?

Overview Your AI-Powered Assistant ▼

Copilot Chat is your AI-powered assistant in Microsoft 365. Access it from Teams (left sidebar), M365.com, or Copilot.com. It can help you draft, summarize, analyze, and create — using your own M365 data.

🔐
Step 1

How to Access Copilot Chat

Setup Three Ways to Get Started ▼
  1. 1 Teams: Click the Copilot sparkle icon in the left navigation rail
  2. 2 Browser: Visit M365.com → then click Copilot
  3. 3 Direct: Visit Copilot.com (sign in with your work account)

Writing Effective Prompts — The GCSE Framework

Framework Prompt Ingredients ▼

A good prompt includes four elements: Goal, Context, Source, and Expectation.

Here is a mnemonic to help you remember the four elements.

Element What It Means Example
Goal What do you want Copilot to do? “Write an email…”
Context Who / what / why? “…to my project team about Q1 progress…”
Source What data should Copilot use? “…based on our last meeting notes…”
Expectation Format, length, and tone? “…in 3 short paragraphs, professional tone.”
Example Weak vs. Strong Prompt ▼
Weak Prompt
Help me with my email.
Strong Prompt
Write a 3-paragraph email to my manager summarizing project status, risks, and next week's priorities. Use a professional tone.

Step 4

Working with Files

How-To How to Attach a File ▼
  1. 1 Click the + (plus) icon in the Copilot Chat prompt bar
  2. 2 Choose from OneDrive, SharePoint, or upload from your device
  3. 3 Select your file — it appears as a chip/badge above the prompt bar
  4. 4 Type your question and press Enter

Supported types: Word (.docx) • PDF (.pdf) • PowerPoint (.pptx) • Excel (.xlsx) • Plain text (.txt)
File size limit: Up to approximately 4 MB per file. Files should be stored in OneDrive or SharePoint for best results.

Cheat Sheet File Prompts Cheat Sheet ▼
What You Want Try This Prompt
Summarize a document “Summarize this in 5 key points” [attach file]
Find action items “What are all the action items and who owns them?” [attach file]
Compare two docs “Compare these two documents and list the key differences” [attach 2 files]
Draft from a file “Based on this brief, draft a client-facing overview in 3 paragraphs” [attach file]
Extract data “List all dates, deadlines, and milestones from this document” [attach file]
Analyze a spreadsheet “Summarize this data and tell me the top 3 trends” [attach Excel]
Rewrite in a new tone “Rewrite this content for a non-technical executive audience” [attach file]

Step 5

Top 10 Power User Tips

Tips Become a Power User ▼
  1. 1 Be specific — include audience, tone, and format in your prompt
  2. 2 Iterate — if the first response isn’t right, refine it (“Make it shorter,” “Use a table”)
  3. 3 Always review — verify facts before sending or acting on Copilot output
  4. 4 Use follow-up questions — Copilot remembers context within a session
  5. 5 Try “What else?” — ask Copilot to expand, add examples, or suggest alternatives
  6. 6 Name your audience — “Write this for a non-technical executive” changes everything
  7. 7 Ask for options — “Give me 3 different versions of this email”
  8. 8 Use it for brainstorming — “Give me 10 ideas for…”
  9. 9 Check privacy — follow your organization’s AI usage policy for sensitive content
  10. 10 Share what works — contribute your best prompts to your team’s shared library
